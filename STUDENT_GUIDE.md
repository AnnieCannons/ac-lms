## 1. Logging In

Go to the app URL and log in with the email and password provided by AnnieCannons.

If you forget your password, use the **Forgot password** link on the login page.

> If you see an instructor dashboard instead of your courses, contact your instructor — your account may need to be updated.

---

## 2. Your Courses

After logging in you will see **My Courses** — a list of all courses you are enrolled in. Click any course to open it.

- A green **Current** badge appears next to courses that are actively running right now
- A blue **TA** badge appears if you have been assigned a Teaching Assistant role for that course — you will see both an **Instructor View** and a **Student View** link for TA courses

---

## 3. Navigating Your Course

Every course page has a **sidebar on the left** with links to all sections of your course. You can collapse the sidebar using the arrow button on its edge if you need more screen space. On mobile, the sidebar is collapsed by default — tap the arrow to expand it.

On mobile, the top-right **hamburger menu** (three lines) gives you access to your Profile and logout.

| Sidebar Item | What you'll find |
|--------------|-----------------|
| **General Info** | Class schedule, policies, resources, and course overview |
| **Syllabus** | The full weekly course outline |
| **Assignments** | All assignments organized by week |
| **My Work** | All assignments in one place, filterable by status |
| **Quizzes** | Published quizzes you can take and retake |
| **Class Resources** | Videos, readings, and links for each week |
| **Career Development** | Career prep content (may also appear on daily coding pages) |
| **Level Up Your Skills** | Extra practice and advanced topics |
| **Benefits** *(paid courses only)* | Healthcare, vision, and dental information |
| **Paid Time Off** *(paid courses only)* | Holidays and school breaks |

---

## 4. Syllabus

The **Syllabus** is your course home page. It shows all modules (weeks) and the days within each week.

- Click a **module** to expand or collapse it
- Click a **day** (e.g. Monday, Tuesday) to see that day's assignments and resources
- The **current week** is highlighted with a badge so you always know where you are
- **Today's day** is highlighted within the current week

---

## 5. General Info

**General Info** contains everything you need to know about your class, including:

- Class schedule and daily routine
- Instructor contact information
- Policies and expectations
- Everyday resources and links your instructor has shared
- Computer and wifi setup instructions
- The yearly schedule — cohort dates, holidays, and school breaks

Sections can be expanded and collapsed. Use **Expand all / Collapse all** at the top to show or hide everything at once.

---

## 6. Assignments

The **Assignments** page lists all assignments organized by week and day.

Each assignment shows:
- **Title**
- **Due date** (if set)
- **Status badge** — whether you have submitted, been graded complete, or need revision

Click any assignment title to open it.

### On the Assignment Page

At the top you will see the assignment title, which module it belongs to, and the due date (if there is one). Below that:

- **Instructions** — read these carefully before starting
- **How to turn this in** — specific steps for submitting your work
- **Instructor Review** *(shown after grading)* — your instructor's checklist with each item checked or unchecked
- **Checklist** *(if the assignment has one)* — see below
- **Submission** — where you turn in your work
- **Comments** *(shown after submitting)* — a thread between you and your instructor

### Checklist
Many assignments include a checklist of requirements. Before submitting:
- Read through each item so you know what is expected
- Check off items as you complete them — this is your own progress tracker and does **not** affect your grade directly
- Your instructor will check off the same items when grading your submission

Items marked **Bonus** are optional — completing them goes above and beyond the requirements.

## 7. Submitting an Assignment

On the assignment page, scroll to the **Submission** section.

### Submission types

| Type | When to use |
|------|-------------|
| **Text** | Written responses, code snippets, short answers |
| **Link** | GitHub repos, deployed sites, Google Docs, Figma links |
| **File** | Uploaded documents or files |

Choose the type that matches what your instructor asked for in the "How to turn this in" section.

### Turning it in

1. Select the submission type
2. Enter your response, paste your link, or upload your file
3. Optionally add a **Note for your instructor** in the text field below your submission — use this to explain context, flag a blocker, or ask a question
4. Click **Submit**

Your submission status will update to **Turned in** and your instructor will be notified. If you left a note, it will appear highlighted on the instructor's grading page.

### Resubmitting

If your instructor marks your work as **Needs Revision**, read their feedback in the comments, make your changes, and submit again using the same form. Your instructor will see your latest submission along with a history of all prior versions.

> You can resubmit as many times as needed until your work is marked **Complete**.

### No-submission assignments

Some assignments (such as live demos or in-class check-offs) do not require you to upload anything. For these you will see a message that says *"No submission needed — your instructor will check this off directly."* Your instructor will mark you complete from their end.

## 8. Understanding Your Grade

After your instructor reviews your work you will see one of two grades:

| Grade | What it means |
|-------|--------------|
| **Complete ✓** | You have met all the required criteria — great work! |
| **Needs Revision** | Some items need more work — check the comments and checklist for details, then resubmit |

The grade badge appears at the top of the assignment page and in your Assignments list.

### Instructor Review Checklist
After grading, an **Instructor Review** section appears on your assignment page showing the checklist with each item marked checked or unchecked. This tells you exactly what your instructor confirmed you completed.

### Comments
Your instructor may leave written feedback in the **Comments** section at the bottom of the assignment page. You can reply to start a conversation. Check back here after submitting — instructors use comments to explain what needs revision.

## 9. My Work

The **My Work** page gives you one place to see every assignment across all weeks of your course.

- Filter by status: **All**, **Not started**, **Turned in**, **Complete**, or **Needs Revision**
- Due dates are shown; assignments past their due date are flagged as Late
- Click any assignment title to open it

This is especially useful near grading deadlines when you want to quickly find everything that still needs your attention.

## 10. Quizzes

Your instructor may publish quizzes alongside your coursework. Find them under **Quizzes** in the sidebar.

### Taking a Quiz

1. Click the quiz title to open it
2. Answer each question — multiple choice or true/false
3. Some questions may include a code snippet to read
4. Click **Submit** when you are done

Your score appears immediately after submitting.

### Retaking a Quiz

If your instructor allows retakes (and you have attempts remaining):
- Click **Retake** on the results screen
- Only questions you got **wrong** are shown on retakes — questions you already answered correctly are locked in

A quiz is locked once you reach the maximum number of attempts. If no limit is set, you can retake as many times as you like.

## 11. Extension Requests

If you need more time on an assignment, you can request an extension directly from the assignment page.

### Submitting a Request

1. Open the assignment and scroll to the **Submission** section
2. Click **Request Extension**
3. Fill in:
   - **Reason** — why you need more time (select from the list)
   - **Proposed due date** — the date you can realistically finish by
   - **Action plan** — what you will do to get caught up
4. Click **Submit Request**

Your instructor will review the request and either approve or deny it. You will receive a notification either way.

### After Your Request is Reviewed

- **Approved** — your due date updates automatically to the date you proposed; the assignment page reflects the new deadline
- **Denied** — you will see the instructor's note explaining why; your original due date remains

> You can have one open extension request per assignment at a time.

## 13. Career Development

The **Career Development** section contains content specifically focused on your professional growth — resume prep, interview skills, networking, and job search resources. This is organized the same way as the Syllabus with expandable modules.

Some Career Development assignments, resources, and quizzes are also cross-posted to appear on your daily coding pages (e.g. Monday of Week 3). When you see a purple **Career Dev** badge on a coding day, that item lives in Career Development but has been pinned here so it stays in context with your other daily work.

## 15. Daily View

Clicking a **day** (e.g. Monday of Week 3) from the Syllabus takes you to that day's detail page, which shows:

- All **resources** for that day with star and complete buttons
- All **assignments** for that day with their current status
- Quick links to open each assignment

This is the most focused view — useful for following along day by day during class.

## 16. Confidence Tracker

The **Confidence Tracker** (found in the top navigation under your profile) lets you rate your own confidence in any skill from 1 to 10.

### Tracking a Skill

1. Click **+ Add Skill** and type the skill name (e.g. *React*, *CSS Flexbox*, *SQL*)
2. Click the skill to open it and log a score — slide the rating to where you honestly feel today
3. Optionally add **goal points** (1–5) to record effort or intention alongside the score
4. Log new scores as often as you like to track growth over time

A chart shows how your confidence has changed over the course. Reviewing this at the end of each week is a great way to notice what clicked and what needs more practice.

## 19. Tips & Common Questions

**I submitted but it still says "Not submitted."**
Try refreshing the page. If the status does not update, check that you clicked **Submit** (not just typed in the box) and that your submission was saved.

**Can I edit my submission after turning it in?**
Yes — you can resubmit at any time using the same form. Your instructor will see your latest submission. All previous versions are saved.

**My assignment says "Late" — can I still submit?**
Yes. The "Late" badge is informational only. You can still submit after the due date. Talk to your instructor if you have concerns about a late submission.

**Where do I find instructor feedback?**
After your instructor grades your work, check the **Comments** section at the bottom of the assignment page and the **Instructor Review** checklist. You may also receive feedback in the grade badge at the top of the page.

**I can't see a module or assignment my instructor mentioned.**
It may not be published yet. Check back later, or ask your instructor when it will be available.

**How do I bookmark a resource to find it again?**
Click the **star icon (☆)** on any resource on the Class Resources page or day detail page. Starred resources are marked with a filled star (★).

**My sidebar is gone.**
Click the **arrow button** on the left edge of the screen to expand the sidebar again.

**I don't see Benefits or Paid Time Off in my sidebar.**
These sections only appear for paid learner courses (Advanced Frontend and Advanced Backend). If you are enrolled in one of those courses and still don't see them, contact your instructor.

**Will I be notified when my work is graded?**
Yes — you will receive an in-app notification (bell icon in the top nav) when your instructor grades a submission or leaves a comment. You will also receive a **daily email digest** around 3 pm that summarizes any new grade results and comments from that day, so you do not have to keep checking the site.

**Who do I contact if I have a technical problem?**
Reach out to your instructor or the AnnieCannons staff through the contact information on the **General Info** page of your course.
